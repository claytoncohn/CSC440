Guidelines

Unless otherwise indicated, all plaintexts and ciphertexts are alphabetic, that is, they only consist of the letters of the alphabet. In general, plaintexts will appear in lowercase and ciphertexts in uppercase. The ciphersystems used here compute with the integer representations of the letters, which are 0 for a, 1 for b, ..., 25 for z.

Negative numbers mod 26 When computing an additive inverse and perhaps in other situations you will get a negative integer. To find that value mod 26, keep adding 26 until the value is in the range 0 to 25 inclusive. For example, the value -95 mod 26 can be found by adding 104 (26 * 4) to -95 to get 9.
